Delicious Egusi Soup Recipe

Egusi Soup is a beloved West African delicacy made with ground melon seeds, rich palm oil, vegetables, and your choice of protein. Popular among Nigerians, especially the Yoruba, Igbo, and Edo tribes, this hearty and flavorful soup is perfect when paired with pounded yam, fufu, or rice. Whether you're new to African cuisine or a seasoned cook, this recipe will guide you to create a pot of soul-satisfying Egusi Soup.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups ground egusi (melon seeds)
  • 1 cup palm oil
  • 500g assorted meat (beef, tripe, cow skin)
  • 200g stock fish (pre-soaked)
  • 200g dried fish (cleaned and deboned)
  • 1 cup ground crayfish
  • 2 tablespoons ground pepper or to taste
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 handfuls of washed bitterleaf or chopped ugu (pumpkin leaves)
  • 1 medium onion (optional, for meat seasoning)
  • 4 cups of meat stock or water

Instructions

  1. Wash and season the assorted meat with salt, seasoning cubes, and onions. Boil until tender. Set aside the meat and reserve the stock.
  2. In a bowl, mix the ground egusi with a small amount of water to form a thick paste.
  3. Heat palm oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the egusi paste in small lumps and allow it to fry, stirring occasionally until it begins to curdle and looks slightly dry (about 10 minutes).
  4. Add the meat stock or water to the egusi and stir well to combine. Let it cook for another 5–10 minutes.
  5. Add the cooked meat, stock fish, and dried fish. Stir and allow to simmer for 10 minutes so the flavors blend.
  6. Add crayfish, ground pepper, and more seasoning to taste. Stir well.
  7. Finally, add the bitterleaf or ugu leaves and allow to simmer for another 5–7 minutes.
  8. Adjust salt and seasoning if necessary. Remove from heat and serve hot with pounded yam, fufu, or rice.
